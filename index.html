Magdalena Fuentes

I'm Assistant Professor of Music Technology and Integrated Design & Media at the Music and Audio Research Lab (MARL) and Integrated Design & Media (IDM) at New York University (NYU). Previosly, I was a Postdoctoral Faculty Fellow at MARL and the Center for Urban Science and Progress (CUSP) at NYU. I did my Ph.D. at Université Paris Saclay in France, in the ADASP group at Télécom ParisTech, and L2S at CentraleSupélec. Before that, I obtained a B.Eng. in Electrical Engineering at Universidad de la República, Uruguay, where I also worked as a research and teaching assistant at the Engineering School and the Music School.

My research interests include Machine Listening, Human-Centered Machine Learning, Multimodal Representation Learning, Self-Supervised Learning, Music Information Retrieval and Environmental Sound Analysis.

Data and software

Soundata: Python library for downloading, loading & working with environmental sound datasets.

Mirdata: Python library for working with Music Information Retrieval datasets.

MONYC: Music of New York City Dataset

SONYC-UST 2.1: Multilabel dataset from an urban acoustic sensor network

SAMBASET: A Brazilian samba dataset of historical samba de enredo recordings

BRID: The Brazilian Rhythmic Instruments Dataset for computational rhythm analysis and source separation

AUCD: The Afro-Uruguayan Candombe Dataset for computational rhythm analysis
